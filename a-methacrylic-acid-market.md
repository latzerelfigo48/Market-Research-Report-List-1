<p><p>The α-Methacrylic Acid market is primarily segmented into two production methods: the Acetone Cyanohydrin Method and the Isobutylene Oxidation Method. The Acetone Cyanohydrin Method involves the reaction of acetone with hydrogen cyanide, leading to the formation of methacrylic acid. In contrast, the Isobutylene Oxidation Method utilizes isobutylene and oxygen, resulting in methacrylic acid through a different catalytic process. Both methods cater to diverse industrial applications, including the production of coatings, adhesives, and plastics, driving market demand.</p></p>
