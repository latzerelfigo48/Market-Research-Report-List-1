<p><p>The X-Ray protective apron market features two main types: lead aprons and lead-free aprons. Lead aprons, traditionally used for radiation protection, contain lead as a shielding material, effectively reducing exposure to harmful X-rays. In contrast, lead-free aprons utilize alternative materials, such as polymers and tungsten, which can offer similar protective benefits while being lighter and more comfortable. Both types are essential in medical and dental environments, ensuring safety for patients and healthcare professionals during radiographic procedures.</p></p>
