<p><p>The neonatal and preterm infants enteral feeding devices market includes several key types. Enteral feeding pumps facilitate controlled delivery of nutrition, essential for vulnerable infants. PEG tubes provide long-term feeding access through a small incision in the abdomen, while nasogastric tubes offer a temporary solution by passing through the nose into the stomach. Gastrostomy tubes are used for direct feeding into the stomach, suitable for infants needing prolonged support. Other devices may include specialized feeding adapters or oral feeding systems, enhancing nutritional intake.</p></p>
